How a fresh permit becomes a job

A permit filing is a moment of maximum intent. The owner has scoped a project, often paid a fee, and is now sequencing the work. Here's the practical playbook:

  • Watch for filings adjacent to your trade. A building permit for an addition or renovation almost always pulls in electrical, plumbing, HVAC, insulation, roofing, and finish work behind it. If you're a sub, the general filing is your signal.
  • Move within days, not weeks. The window between filing and hiring subs is short. Contractors who reach the owner while decisions are still open win disproportionately.
  • Reference the actual project. "I saw you pulled a permit for work at your property" is a fundamentally different opener than a generic mailer. It's specific, credible, and relevant on day one.
  • Mind homeowner-pulled permits. When an owner files without a contractor listed, that often means they haven't hired one yet — or plan to DIY part of the job and hire out the rest. Either way, it's the warmest kind of lead.
