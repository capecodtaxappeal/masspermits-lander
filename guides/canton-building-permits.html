How a fresh permit becomes a job

A permit filing is the earliest public signal that a property owner is spending money. The owner has committed to a project, hired at least one contractor, and — critically — is about to discover everything else the project needs. The homeowner who just pulled a building permit on Elizabeth Street will likely need an electrician, a plumber, and possibly a gas fitter within weeks. The one who filed for a gas permit may be mid-heating-conversion and shopping for related work.

Reaching an owner right after they file wins work for a simple reason: you're arriving while decisions are still being made. There's no bidding war yet, no three-quotes ritual — just a homeowner in project mode who's relieved a qualified local contractor reached out. That's why permit-based Canton construction leads convert better than cold lists: every name has already proven intent by filing with the town.
