Cohasset Building Permits: What 651 Recent Filings Tell Contractors

Where the work is in Cohasset right now

We're tracking 651 recent building permits in Cohasset, MA, and the trade mix tells a clear story: this is a renovation town, not a new-construction town. Electrical leads with 188 permits, with Renovation/Remodel right behind at 184 — together those two categories account for more than half of all recent filings. Plumbing (105) and Gas (94) round out the core, while standalone Building permits sit at just 29. In other words, Cohasset homeowners are opening up and upgrading the houses they already own — and every one of those projects pulls in multiple licensed trades.

That pattern fits the housing stock. Recent permits cluster along coastal and village streets like Jerusalem Rd, Atlantic Ave, Border St, North Main St, and South Main St, plus corridors like C J Cushing Hwy and Beechwood St. Older shoreline homes need rewiring, re-plumbing, and gas conversions when they get remodeled — which is exactly what the numbers show.

The busiest trades in Cohasset

  • Electrical (188) — the single largest category. Panel upgrades, remodel wiring, and service work dominate.
  • Renovation/Remodel (184) — nearly tied for first. Kitchens, baths, and whole-home updates are the engine of Cohasset's construction economy.
  • Plumbing (105) and Gas (94) — the natural downstream of all that remodel activity.
  • Building (29), Other (20), and HVAC (17) — smaller slices, with HVAC the least-filed tracked trade here.

How a fresh permit becomes your next job

A building permit is the earliest public signal that a Cohasset homeowner has committed money to a project. When a Renovation/Remodel permit gets filed on Jerusalem Rd or Bayberry Ln, that project will almost certainly need an electrician, a plumber, and often a gas fitter or HVAC contractor within weeks — and many of those subs haven't been chosen yet. The general contractor may also need help, and homeowners running their own projects definitely do.

Reaching that owner right after they file beats every other lead channel for one simple reason: intent is already proven. They aren't "thinking about" a project — they've paid a permit fee and scheduled work. A contractor who introduces themselves at that moment isn't cold-calling; they're showing up exactly when the homeowner is assembling a team. That's why permit-based Cohasset construction leads convert at a fraction of the cost of shared lead platforms, where five contractors chase the same tire-kicker.
