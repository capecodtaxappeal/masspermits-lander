How a Dedham permit becomes your next job

Every permit filed with the Town of Dedham is a public record with a project address, an owner, usually a contractor of record, and a project type. That record tells you three things a cold list never can: who is spending money right now, on what, and where. An owner on Walters Avenue who just pulled an HVAC permit has an open project — and open projects sprawl. The furnace swap surfaces a corroded gas line, an undersized panel, attic insulation that should have been done a decade ago.

The window matters. Reach a Dedham owner the week they file and you are talking to someone mid-decision, often before adjacent work has been bid. Reach them a month later and the checkbook is closed. That is the core reason permit-based Dedham construction leads outperform door-knocking and bought lists: the intent is documented, dated, and tied to a specific address you can drive past on the way to your next job.
