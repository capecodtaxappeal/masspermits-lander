How a fresh permit becomes a job

A permit filing is the single strongest buying signal in residential construction. The owner has already decided to spend, already engaged the town, and is now assembling the rest of the project. A homeowner on Jay Dee Lane or Oak Leaf Road who just pulled a building permit will shortly need the trades that permit doesn't cover — and most of them haven't picked those contractors yet.

The timing edge is everything. Reach that owner in the first days after filing and you're a helpful professional showing up at the exact moment they're making hiring decisions. Reach them a month later and the subs are booked. That's why permit-driven outreach converts so much better than general advertising: you're not persuading someone to start a project, you're offering to finish one they've already committed to.
