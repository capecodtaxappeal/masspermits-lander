Hanson, MA Building Permits: What 490 Recent Filings Tell Contractors

Hanson is a renovation town. Of the 490 recent building permits we track here, 203 are renovation and remodel jobs — more than any other category, and more than plumbing, gas, and HVAC combined. That single number tells you where the work is in this corner of Plymouth County: homeowners on streets like Whitman St, Winter St, and East Washington St are opening up their existing houses rather than building new ones.

The Hanson trade mix, by the numbers

  • Renovation/Remodel — 203 permits. The clear leader. Kitchens, additions, and interior work dominate Hanson's filing activity.
  • Electrical — 127 permits. The second-busiest trade, which fits the renovation-heavy pattern: nearly every remodel pulls a separate electrical permit.

How a fresh permit becomes a job

A building permit is the single strongest buying signal in residential construction. When a Hanson homeowner files, they've already committed money and decided to do the project — the only open question is who does the connected work.

  • A remodel permit means sub-trades get hired next. The homeowner or GC needs licensed electrical, plumbing, gas, and HVAC contractors, usually on a short timeline.
  • One project reveals the next. A homeowner opening walls for a remodel often discovers panel upgrades, re-piping, or heating replacements they didn't plan for.
  • Neighbors follow neighbors. Visible work on a street like Main St or East Washington St reliably generates nearby inquiries — being the contractor already on the street is the cheapest marketing there is.
