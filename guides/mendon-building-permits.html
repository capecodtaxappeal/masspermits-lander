Mendon is a small town, but its permit desk is anything but quiet. We are currently tracking 111 recent building permits in Mendon, MA — and the mix tells a clear story about where the work is landing.

What Mendon's Trade Mix Says

Of the 111 permits tracked, Building leads with 41 — more than a third of all filings, and the largest single category by a wide margin. That is the signature of a town in renovation and addition mode: decks, remodels, basement finishes, and structural work on the colonials and newer subdivision homes that line roads like Dudley Road, Butler Road, and Locust Hill Drive.

When gas, plumbing, and HVAC filings cluster this closely together, it usually means system replacements and conversions — water heaters, boiler swaps, mini-splits, and gas line work — are running steadily alongside the bigger renovation projects.

How a Fresh Permit Becomes Your Next Job

A building permit is the single strongest buying signal a homeowner produces. Someone on Esty Road or Stymast Drive who just filed has already committed money, scheduled inspections, and started a project — and projects sprawl. The homeowner who pulled a building permit for an addition will shortly need an electrician, a plumber, an HVAC installer, and often a painter and flooring crew. In Mendon's current mix, with 41 building permits feeding demand into the mechanical trades, that downstream work is exactly where subcontractors win.

Timing is everything. Reach that owner the week they file and you are a helpful local pro arriving at the right moment. Reach them a month later and the subs are already booked.
