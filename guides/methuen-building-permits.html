Where the work is: Methuen's trade mix

Among the recent Methuen building permits we track, the busiest categories are:

  • Additions — 47 permits. The single largest category. Methuen homeowners are adding square footage rather than moving, which means framing, foundation, roofing, electrical, and finish work all downstream of each filing.
  • Pool/Spa — 36 permits. A striking second place. Every pool permit pulls in excavation, fencing, electrical bonding, and often patio and landscaping work.
  • Building — 29 permits. General building work spanning remodels and structural projects.
  • Solar — 25 permits. Rooftop installs keep filing steadily, and each one can surface roofing and electrical-panel upgrade work.
  • Gas — 24 permits and HVAC — 24 permits. Tied mechanical categories, often connected to the same conversion or system-replacement projects.
  • Plumbing — 22 permits. Rounding out the mix, frequently paired with kitchen and bath remodels.

The pattern worth noticing: additions and pools together outnumber every mechanical trade. That's discretionary, high-ticket work — the kind of projects where owners hire multiple contractors over several months.

How a fresh permit becomes your next job

A building permit is the single strongest buying signal in residential construction. When a Methuen homeowner files for an addition or a pool, you know three things for certain: they own the property, they've committed budget, and a project is starting now — not "someday."

Most permits also create work beyond the trade on the filing. An addition permit means a homeowner who will soon need HVAC extended, plumbing roughed in, and siding matched. A pool permit means fencing and hardscape. The contractor who reaches that owner first — while the project is live and decisions are still being made — wins the adjacent work without competing against ten other bids.
