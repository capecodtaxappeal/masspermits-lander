Solar punching above its weight

Solar at 25 filings edging out gas and plumbing is the most interesting line in the Orleans data. A demolition filing in Orleans is rarely the end of a story. Tight lot coverage rules and conservation setbacks mean owners who tear down almost always rebuild on the footprint. How a fresh permit becomes your next job

Orleans building permits are public records, but by the time they surface in annual reports the work is done. The window that matters is the first days after filing:

  • A permit is proof of budget. The owner has already committed money and cleared the town's process — this is not a tire-kicker.
  • Most projects need more trades than the permit names. A building permit on Tonset Rd typically pulls in plumbing, gas, HVAC, and electrical work behind it. The gas/plumbing tie at 24 each in the data shows how tightly those trades track the building work.
  • Reaching the owner early wins referral position. An owner mid-project on Shore View Dr who hears from a local HVAC or solar contractor before the GC has locked in subs can still shape who does the work — and Cape owners talk to their neighbors.
