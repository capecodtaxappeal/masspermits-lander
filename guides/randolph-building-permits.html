The trade mix in Randolph's permit data

Here's how the 900 recent Randolph building permits break down by trade:

  • Renovation/remodel — 358 permits. The dominant category. Interior work drives Randolph's permit activity.
  • Electrical — 257 permits. The second-busiest trade, and a natural companion to all that remodel work: panel upgrades, rewiring, new circuits.

The takeaway: renovation/remodel and electrical together account for well over half of all recent filings. If you work either trade, Randolph should be on your route.

How a fresh permit becomes your next job

A building permit is the single strongest buying signal in residential construction. Most projects don't stop at one trade — a remodel permit today often means electrical, plumbing, and HVAC needs within weeks.

The contractor who reaches that owner first has an enormous edge. You're not cold-calling someone who might renovate someday; you're introducing yourself to a homeowner mid-project who may need exactly your trade next, or who hit a snag with their current crew.
