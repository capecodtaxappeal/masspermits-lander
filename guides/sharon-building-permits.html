Sharon Building Permits: Where the Work Is Right Now

What Sharon's permit activity says about the town

Sharon is a lake town, and its permit sheet proves it. Among the 181 recent permits we track here, electrical work leads everything with 41 filings — but the number that jumps out is Pool/Spa at 30, the second-busiest trade in town. That is unusual for a Massachusetts suburb, and it fits Sharon's profile: larger lots around Lake Massapoag, established single-family neighborhoods, and homeowners investing in outdoor living. A pool permit is rarely a solo project — it pulls electrical (bonding and equipment circuits), gas (heaters), and often fencing and hardscape behind it, which helps explain why electrical sits on top of the stack.

The rest of the mix is steady whole-house upkeep: plumbing (24), HVAC (22), gas (22), roofing (21), and solar (21). Solar matching roofing filing-for-filing is worth noting — Sharon's housing stock is old enough that reroofs and panel installs frequently travel together, and a roofer who partners with a solar installer (or vice versa) can capture both sides of the same roof.

Where the filings are coming from

Recent permits cluster on streets that span the whole town: Wolomolopoag St and Massapoag Ave near the lake, Briar Hill Rd, Pond St, Tisdale Rd, King Philip Rd, and Lyndon Rd among others. These are established residential streets — not new subdivisions — which means the work is renovation, replacement, and upgrade driven. That is exactly the kind of demand that repeats: a street that files a roofing permit this month tends to file more of them, because the houses went up in the same era and age together.

How a fresh permit becomes your next job

A building permit is the earliest public signal that a homeowner is spending money on their house. For contractors chasing jobs in Sharon MA, that signal matters for two reasons:

  • Follow-on work. The permit that was filed is rarely the whole project. A pool permit means electrical, gas, and fencing are coming. A solar permit often means the roof was — or needs to be — addressed. An HVAC changeout can surface electrical panel upgrades.
  • A qualified, in-motion owner. Someone who just pulled a permit has already decided to spend. They have a project manager mindset this month. Reaching them now, with a relevant adjacent service, beats cold-calling a list of strangers who have no active project.

Timing is the whole game. Permit data goes stale fast — the electrician who sees a Pool/Spa filing on Massapoag Ave this week can introduce themselves before the pool builder's preferred sub locks up the work. Three months later, that lead is worthless.

Who should be watching Sharon filings

  • Electricians — the busiest trade in town, and the trade every other permit type feeds.
  • Pool and outdoor-living contractors — 30 recent pool/spa permits is a dense, unusual concentration for a town this size.
  • Roofers and solar installers — dead-even at 21 filings each, and natural cross-referral partners.
  • Plumbers and HVAC/gas contractors — steady replacement-cycle demand across the older housing stock.

Sharon construction leads reward specialists who move quickly. The permit log tells you the street, the trade, and the timing — the rest is a knock, a postcard, or a phone call while the project is still live.

FAQ

Why are pool permits such a big deal for contractors in Sharon?

With 30 recent pool/spa permits, Sharon shows a concentration of outdoor-living investment rare for a suburb its size. How fast should I act on a new Sharon building permit lead?
