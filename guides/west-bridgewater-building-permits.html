How a fresh permit becomes a paying job

A building permit is the single strongest buying signal in residential construction. The owner has committed money, hired at least one contractor, and started a project that almost always needs more hands than the original filing covers.

  • Remodels create trade openings. A homeowner who pulls a renovation permit on Matfield St will need electrical, plumbing, and often gas or HVAC work permitted separately — sometimes weeks later. Reaching them before those follow-on permits are filed puts you first in line.
  • Active projects reveal active spenders. An owner mid-renovation is the most likely person on the street to green-light the next project — a panel upgrade, a bathroom, a heating conversion.
  • Timing is everything. Once a permit is a few weeks old, the subs are chosen. The week of filing is when an introduction still wins work.
